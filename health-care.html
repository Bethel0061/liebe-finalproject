General Health Assessments
Vital Signs Monitoring: Volunteers measure and record patients' blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, and respiration rates to assess their general health.
Symptom Evaluation: They ask patients about symptoms, medical history, and any current health concerns to identify potential health issues.
Treatment of Common Illnesses
Basic Medical Care: Volunteers diagnose and treat common illnesses such as respiratory infections, gastrointestinal issues, fevers, and skin conditions.
Medication Administration: They dispense prescribed medications, explain dosage instructions, and monitor patients for side effects.
Health Education and Preventive Care
Hygiene Promotion: Volunteers teach proper hygiene practices, such as handwashing and safe food preparation, to prevent disease.
Nutrition Education: They provide guidance on healthy eating habits, especially for children and pregnant women, to combat malnutrition.
Disease Prevention: Volunteers educate communities about preventing common diseases like malaria, HIV/AIDS, and tuberculosis through workshops and informational sessions.
Mental Health Support
Counseling: Volunteers offer basic counseling services to individuals dealing with stress, anxiety, or trauma.
Support Groups: They may facilitate or support group sessions for those affected by mental health issues, providing a safe space to share and heal.
